"Sign Language Is A Human Right Of Deaf"

Introduction to Sign Language

Sign Language is develop for deaf community of India, which can be used as a mean of communication between friends and families of deaf people and the deaf people.

There are two types of sign language:

  1. Alternate Sign Language: It is a system of hand signals developed by speakers for limited communication in a specific context where speech cananot be used.
  2. Primary Sign Language: It is the language of a group of people who do not use a spoken language with each other.

Who uses Sign Language?

The class of people that use sign language include the following:

  • Deaf
  • Sign Language Newscasters
  • Some parents also teach their babies sign language becoase it enhances their communciation skills

Why is Sign Language Important?

Sign language is important to people that cannot communicate verbally because of the following reasons:

  • Have the right to exercise their civil as well as linguistic-cultural rights,
  • Have the right to pursue studies/education in sign-language,
  • Have the right to equal and, full access to information as well as accessible communication and orientation,
  • Have the right to assert/represent themselves in sihn-language and utilize the right to vote,
  • Have the right to use sign-language in all areas of life.

Indian Sign Language (ISL)

Sign language has evolved in India over the last 100 years, but the government decided to codify it in the form of a dictionary in 2017. Indian Sign Language Research and Training Centre (ISLRTC) launched the first Indian Sign Language Dictionary of 3000 terms on 23rd March, 2018 at India International Center, New Delhi and inaugurated the 3rd Edition of Indian Sign Language Dictionary with 10,000 terms on 17th February 2021 in an online programme.

The Dictionary, released in DVD form, contains signs of everyday use and their corresponding English and Hindi words. Specialized terms from legal, academic, medical, and technical fields are explained in ISL. The Dictionary will benefit interpreters, teachers of the deaf, parents of deaf children, etc. and will also help deaf adults to learn English/Hindi.
